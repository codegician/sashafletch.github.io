About Me

My road to UX Design has been filled with a lot of twists and turns, but my background in psychology, anthropology, and education has informed my design experience, helping me to build creative and empathetic solutions for users.

Whether I was seeing psychology through a social lens or a neurological lens, I was consistently fascinated by the influences behind individual behavior and thinking. So I intended to become a psychotherapist or work in psychology research because of the field’s focus on improving other’s lived experiences through communication and analysis.

However, I soon discovered I preferred UX design over traditional psychology research; UX seeks to provide research that can inform decision making and directly influence the experiences of the everyday user. It also doesn’t completely isolate research from practice; UX covers everything that initially inspired me within my undergraduate education.
